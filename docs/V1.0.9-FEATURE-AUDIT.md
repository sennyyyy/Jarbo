# ALPHA Jarbo 1.0.9 Feature Audit

## Purpose

This document is the source of truth for what works well, what works with limitations, what does not work, and what is intentionally excluded from the ALPHA Jarbo 1.0.9 closeout.

Do not mark a feature as verified from source inspection or a previous release note alone. A verified result requires a completed test on the v1.0.9 release candidate with the environment and evidence recorded below.

Current decision: **HOLD**. Candidate `58246c2` passes fresh-clone automation and exact-candidate CI, but Part B exposed release-blocking lifecycle failures. The canonical RC produced two `EXC_BAD_ACCESS` crashes in menu update and close/reopen paths (JARBO-109-003/004), and menu-bar Quit remains disabled (JARBO-109-001). Part C is complete with documented CPU/recovery limitations (JARBO-109-005/006). Part D remains incomplete with unsafe recognition/occlusion failures (JARBO-109-007/008/009/010). Part E is in progress: E01 contains Critical unintended clicks and poor pointer control (JARBO-109-011/012), E05 found an unstable/premature right-button hold (JARBO-109-013), and E06 found thumb/ring-to-Finger-Heart collision (JARBO-109-014). Later safety/performance/soak evidence remains pending.

## Status definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|

| Pass | Works consistently and meets its acceptance criteria. |

## Release summary

Complete this section after testing.

### Works well

- Fresh-clone verification passes 13/13 tests and produces a signed universal app.
- The verified app launches as one process with one visible HUD and no immediate crash.
- An isolated new profile launches with Camera Off and no camera privacy indication.
- Jarbo appears and can be selected through the Dock, Command-Tab, and Mission Control/F3 without duplicating the HUD.
- Ordinary HUD hide/show, focus preservation, and full-screen/Space coexistence passed the human Part B observation.

### Works with limitations

- The dedicated menu-bar icon, light/dark appearance, accessibility label, menu contents, and Camera Off behavior pass, but `Quit Jarbo` is disabled.
- The extended Part A launch session emitted one non-crashing SwiftUI `AttributeGraph` cycle warning with no observed visual failure; reproduction and impact remain unknown.
- The 30-minute background behavior was reported as good, but no CPU/memory trace was attached.
- Ten camera cycles completed without duplicate sessions or repeated memory growth, but Camera-Off CPU remained approximately 49–55%.

### Does not work

- `Quit Jarbo` appears in the menu-bar menu but is greyed out and cannot be clicked (JARBO-109-001).
- The canonical app has crashed while opening/updating its menu and while restoring after a closed HUD (JARBO-109-003/004).

### Blocked or untested

- All camera/gesture, permission-transition, application-action, performance, and soak cases remain untested on the designated release candidate.

### Intentionally excluded

- Voice controls and speech-command reliability.
- PDF reading and PDF understanding.
- Image reading and visual-content understanding.
- The 3D model viewer and related rendering/content workflows.
