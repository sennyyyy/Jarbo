# ALPHA Jarbo 1.0.6

## Working

- Replaced generic pose-based clicking with a dedicated three-contact detector for left-thumb-to-index, left-thumb-to-middle, and left-thumb-to-ring pinches.
- Index pinch defaults to held left click, middle pinch to held right click, and ring pinch to held middle click.
- Each contact uses palm-normalized distance, closest-finger arbitration, two-frame acquisition, fist-region rejection, and a wider release threshold for stable holding.
- Pinch holds emit native mouse-down and drag events continuously; finger separation, hand loss, tracking stop, or app termination emits mouse-up.
- Increased fingertip landmark responsiveness while retaining stronger smoothing on palm and knuckle anchors.
- Personal contact samples remain available as a strict fallback, but researched priors are prohibited from triggering click contacts.
- Reordered gesture arbitration to dedicated contact/motion detection, personal templates, deterministic geometry, researched priors, then explicit No Gesture.
- Removed the old behavior that classified every unmatched pose as Point.
- Added a real No Gesture state so ambiguous frames cannot silently fire a control.
- Increased static-action confirmation from two to three stable frames while keeping pointer movement and deliberate pinch/motion events responsive.
- Tightened researched-prior confidence and separation margins to reduce collisions between similar gestures.
- Added schema 7 migration and an editable default Thumb + Ring → Middle click binding. Existing custom ring-pinch assignments are preserved without adding a conflicting second mouse button.

## Known limitations

- A monocular webcam still cannot see contact hidden fully behind another finger. Keep the thumb/fingertip contact visible to the camera and use even front lighting.
- Personal samples of Finger Heart, Thumb + Middle, and Thumb + Ring may act as a strict fallback, but the dedicated geometric contact detector remains the primary click path.
- Similar complex static configurations still benefit from completing all 10 personal samples.
- Gesture quality depends on full hand visibility, camera resolution, and avoiding severe motion blur.
- Mac control still requires Accessibility and, for desktop switching, System Events Automation permission.
- The downloadable build remains x86_64, ad-hoc signed, and not notarized.
