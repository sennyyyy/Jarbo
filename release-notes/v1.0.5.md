# ALPHA Jarbo 1.0.5

## Working

- Expanded Personal Training to the requested catalog of 20 static finger configurations, 20 dynamic motions, and 10 hand/wrist orientations.
- Every gesture now owns an independent bank of up to 10 samples and displays its own 0/10 progress count.
- Static gestures use palm-size, wrist-position, and rotation-normalized 21-point landmark features.
- Orientation gestures preserve wrist rotation instead of normalizing it away.
- Dynamic gestures use a resampled 1.3-second trajectory containing wrist movement, index path, palm-size change, finger openness, and pinch distance.
- Added trained trajectory recognition for waves, circles, shapes, push/pull, air taps, air clicks, grab/release, and shake motions.
- Added generic recognition for horizontal and vertical swipes plus additional static finger configurations; complex configurations can be personalized with their own samples.
- Researched primary hand-gesture datasets and generated 720 compact static priors from 360 HaGRIDv2 test annotations plus mirrored variants across 12 trustworthy label mappings.
- Bundled priors contain normalized numerical hand landmarks only—no photographs, user IDs, personal metadata, or image identifiers.
- Personal samples have priority: once a gesture has a personal sample, Jarbo stops using the bundled HaGRID priors for that gesture.
- Added the HaGRID attribution/share-alike notice and a reproducible prior-generation tool.

## Known limitations

- HaGRID priors cover 12 confidently matched static classes, not all 50 requested gestures. The remaining static classes and all dynamic/orientation classes require personal samples because unrelated internet labels would reduce accuracy.
- Train all 10 samples for similar-looking configurations such as OK, Finger Heart, Thumb + Middle, and Thumb + Ring.
- For a dynamic gesture, perform the motion first and immediately press **Add Recent Motion**; the saved sample covers the preceding 1.3 seconds.
- A monocular webcam and 2D Vision landmarks cannot reliably infer true palm depth. Palm Up/Down and Palm/Back Facing Camera benefit strongly from ten varied personal samples and may remain less reliable than finger configurations.
- GPT is used for research and label/schema design, not inside the real-time control loop. Network inference is too latent and nondeterministic for frame-level mouse control.
- Gesture quality still depends on lighting, full hand visibility, and limited finger occlusion.
- Jarbo still requires Accessibility and System Events Automation permission for Mac control.
- The downloadable build remains x86_64, ad-hoc signed, and not notarized.
